Field Story · Bhopal Pilot · Night Shift

It was 9:15 PM on a Tuesday in Bhopal.

A pharmacist named Vikram had just dispensed a painkiller. The patient's handwritten prescription said "Diclofenac 50mg." What it didn't say — what Vikram had no way of knowing — was that the patient was already on a blood thinner.

The drug interaction would have caused internal bleeding. SetuAI's scanner flagged it in 4 seconds.

That one alert recovered ₹31,000 in liability for one pharmacy in our 47-store Bhopal pilot. It also, almost certainly, saved someone from a hospital visit they didn't know they were heading toward.

We aren't building a billing app. We're building the AI Co-Pilot that makes the neighborhood chemist the safest place to get healthcare in India.
